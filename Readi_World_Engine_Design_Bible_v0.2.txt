CORE PROMISE

“A small world that remembers you, visibly grows with you, and always feels good to return to.”

PURPOSE

This document is the central source of truth for Readi World. Separate conversations may design characters,
locations, buildings, animations, systems, UI, code or art, but all work must remain compatible with this Bible.

VERSION 0.2 CHANGE SUMMARY

Version 0.2 replaces several earlier prototype assumptions after real-device testing.

Changed locked decisions:
- Primary orientation changed from landscape to portrait.
- The fixed left joystick was replaced by a floating joystick that appears where the player touches.
- The permanent ACT button was removed.
- Harvestable resources use automatic proximity interaction where appropriate.
- Buildings use one contextual prompt at a time.
- The top HUD now follows the approved B+ layout.
- Important buildings, entrances and paths require procedural clear zones.
- Permanent object-name labels are removed from normal gameplay.
- GitHub Pages and Home Screen PWA are now the preferred daily test environment.
- The current logical viewport is 720 x 1280.
- The initial village layout is designed around a readable vertical flow.

Unchanged foundations:
- One persistent home village.
- Compact scenes and replayable stages.
- Atlas-based visuals.
- Layered character equipment.
- Automatic categorized inventory.
- Optional combat.
- Four-hour world day.
- Automatic persistent saving.
- Deterministic generation.
- English game and code.
- Audio-ready semantic events.
- Coming Soon as an intentional product state.
- No false claim of cheat-proof security without a backend.

CHAPTER 01 — VISION AND NON-NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLES

LOCKED DECISION
Readi World is a cozy, progression-focused portrait mobile game centered on one evolving home village.
It respects player time, minimizes administration and does not require combat.

The desired emotion is ownership without pressure. The player should feel that the village is personally theirs,
that short visits matter, that the world remembers changes and that visible progression is more important than
menu-driven numbers.

Two minutes of play should produce a gathered resource, visible environmental change, progress toward an upgrade,
a discovered route, useful goal information or a pleasant world-state change.

Anti-goals:
- tower defense;
- idle economy simulation;
- heavy farming simulation;
- inventory-management simulation;
- forced combat;
- giant continuously loaded open world;
- notification-driven obligation;
- pay-to-win or frustration-to-payment design.

Decision filter:
- Does the idea strengthen ownership, discovery, visible growth or calm mastery?
- Does it work comfortably in portrait?
- Does it respect a two-minute session?
- Does it create chores that should be automated?
- Does it force one specialization?
- Can it reuse a general system?
- Can the player understand it without excessive UI?

CHAPTER 02 — PLAYER EXPERIENCE AND CORE LOOP

LOCKED DECISION
The player always returns to one persistent home village. Activities branch outward and feed visible improvements
back into the village.

Primary loop:
1. Leave the village for a compact area or stage.
2. Gather resources, discover something or complete a small objective.
3. Return home.
4. Resources enter automatic categorized storage.
5. Spend resources on construction, upgrades, tools or decoration.
6. See the village change.
7. Choose the next specialization.

Session expectations:
- 2 minutes: harvest, check growth or make one small improvement.
- 10 minutes: complete a compact stage or gather toward an upgrade.
- 30 minutes: progress an area tier or unlock a meaningful feature.
- Long play: chain activities freely without energy walls.

Combat remains optional and must never block peaceful progression.

CHAPTER 03 — WORLD STRUCTURE AND SCENE MODEL

LOCKED DECISION
The world is a set of compact scenes, not one giant continuously simulated map.

Village Hub branches:
- Farm
- Forest stages
- Mine stages
- Harbor and Sea stages
- Mountains — future
- Expedition Gate — future

The village is persistent and mostly hand-authored. Activity areas may be authored, procedural or hybrid.
Only the current scene is fully rendered and updated.

LOCKED DECISION — PORTRAIT FLOW
The village is composed for readable vertical navigation.

Recommended broad layout:
- upper area: Forest and Mine;
- upper-middle: Forester’s Hut and woodland landmarks;
- center: Home and main path;
- lower-middle: Farm, well and service buildings;
- lower area: Harbor and Expedition access.

The first camera view should show the player, Home, a path and at least one destination.

Current logical viewport: 720 x 1280.
Current village planning size: 1344 x 1920 logical pixels.
Planning tile: 48 x 48 logical pixels.

Area progression examples:
Harbor: Beach -> Shallow Water -> Open Sea -> Deep Ocean -> Islands -> Frozen Sea
Mine: Surface -> Copper -> Iron -> Gold -> Crystal -> Ancient Ruins -> Magic Depths
Forest: Clearing -> Pine Forest -> Old Forest -> Swamp -> Enchanted Grove

Every tier introduces a meaningful new resource, rule, verb, encounter or visual identity.

Village specialization remains player-driven. Harbor, mining and farming identities must all be viable.

CHAPTER 04 — TIME, DAY CYCLE AND OFFLINE PROGRESSION

LOCKED DECISION
The world clock advances in real time while open or closed, but is independent from the player’s local day/night.

Four-hour world day:
- Hour 1: Morning
- Hour 2: Afternoon
- Hour 3: Evening
- Hour 4: Night

The cycle then returns to Morning.

World time model:
worldTimeMs = savedWorldTimeMs + max(0, nowMs - lastRealTimestampMs)

The top HUD shows phase icon, phase name, world day and in-game clock.
Example: Morning — Day 1 · 08:30

Allowed offline:
- crop growth;
- tree regrowth;
- construction;
- day-phase progress;
- natural environmental changes.

Not allowed offline:
- active harvesting;
- infinite currency production;
- quest decisions;
- combat completion;
- player movement;
- dangerous expedition completion.

Phone-time manipulation cannot be fully prevented without a backend. Detect and clamp suspicious jumps rather
than punish players aggressively.

CHAPTER 05 — SAVING, PERSISTENCE AND RECOVERY

LOCKED DECISION
Anything the player changes, wherever they stop, is remembered automatically.

Save:
- player position, direction and scene;
- resources and currency;
- object states;
- harvested trees and respawn times;
- building levels and construction;
- world seed and time anchor;
- equipment and appearance;
- settings;
- milestones and progression;
- save version and game version.

Save triggers:
- important interaction;
- harvest completion;
- upgrade;
- scene transition;
- periodic debounced autosave;
- page hidden or app backgrounded;
- import/export;
- migration.

Save layers:
- current save;
- backup save;
- previous backup when possible.

Current prototype may use localStorage. The SaveManager API must remain storage-independent for later migration
to IndexedDB or cloud save.

The game must load v0.0.1 and v0.0.2 data safely. Stable IDs are never reused for a different meaning.

CHAPTER 06 — ENGINE ARCHITECTURE

LOCKED DECISION
The prototype may remain one index.html, but internal sections behave like separable modules.

Engine systems:
GameLoop, Renderer, AtlasManager, SceneManager, ObjectManager, CollisionSystem, InteractionSystem,
InputManager, Camera, AnimationSystem, TimeSystem, SaveManager, AudioManager, EventBus, Localization,
UIManager and DebugTools.

Game content:
Village, Farming, Forest, Mining, Harbor, Fishing, Milestones, Quests and NPCs.
Future systems remain reserved until activated by the roadmap.

Main-loop rules:
- clamp delta time;
- never simulate offline progress through giant frame steps;
- pause expensive work while hidden;
- render visible objects only.

Semantic event examples:
- audio.emit("tree.chop")
- events.emit("resource.collected", payload)
- events.emit("building.enter", payload)
- events.emit("building.upgraded", payload)
- events.emit("save.requested", payload)

CHAPTER 07 — WORLD OBJECTS, COMPONENTS AND STATES

LOCKED DECISION
Trees, rocks, crops, buildings, boats and interactables share one object format.

Reference fields:
id, type, subtype, transform, visual, collision, interaction, autoAction, clearRadius, state, level,
persistent, audioProfile and tags.

Components:
- Transform: position, scale, direction, depth anchor.
- Visual: atlas, cell, animation, tint, visibility.
- Collision: physical footprint.
- Interaction: type, trigger, range, label, priority and requirements.
- Harvest: resource, amount, duration or hit count and regeneration.
- Growth: stage and timestamps.
- Upgrade: level, cost and construction.
- Clear zone: decoration-free radius.
- Persistence: serialized state.

Player states: idle, walking, chopping, mining, fishing, interacting, transitioning and disabled.
Tree states: healthy, chopping, falling, stump and regrowing.
Building states: locked, available, constructing, active and upgrading.

CHAPTER 08 — RENDERING, LAYERS, ATLASES AND PIVOTS

LOCKED DECISION
The production visual pipeline is atlas-based with standardized pivots and metadata.

World layers:
0 Ground
10 Paths and soil
20 Ground decoration
30 Crops and low objects
40 Buildings and trunks
50 Characters and moving objects
60 Canopies and foreground occlusion
70 Weather and lighting
80 Context highlights
90 Context prompts
100 Fixed UI

Depth-sensitive drawing uses foot/pivot Y.

Separate atlases for character base, hair, clothes, tools, trees, plants, rocks, houses, harbor, boats and UI.

Atlas rules:
- transparent PNG;
- exact cell dimensions;
- consistent perspective and light direction;
- consistent ground line;
- safety padding;
- no labels, numbers or grid lines;
- metadata for row, column, pivot, collision and animation.

Missing assets use a visible debug fallback and a neutral player-mode fallback.

CHAPTER 09 — CHARACTER, CLOTHING, TOOLS AND ANIMATION

LOCKED DECISION
The character is assembled from synchronized layers.

Layer stack:
shadow, body base, pants, boots, torso, head, hair, tool or weapon, hat/accessory and foreground effect.

All layers share exact frame dimensions, direction order, pivot, pose and timing.

Foundation states:
- idle;
- walking;
- chopping;
- direction tracking.

Future states:
- mining;
- fishing;
- watering;
- combat.

Changing an axe, shirt or hat swaps only the relevant layer.

CHAPTER 10 — VILLAGE, BUILDINGS, BOATS AND VISUAL PROGRESSION

LOCKED DECISION
Village progression must be visually obvious.

Building methods:
- full-state atlas when silhouette changes;
- layered building when the base remains stable;
- hybrid when major levels change the base and cosmetics remain layered.

LOCKED DECISION — BUILDING INTERACTION
Buildings do not display permanent names. When the player enters range, one contextual pill appears.
Examples: Enter Home, Visit Farm, Visit Forester’s Hut, Inspect Mine and Inspect Harbor.
Only one primary prompt may be visible.

Construction transition:
- enter constructing state;
- show scaffolding or materials;
- advance with world time if appropriate;
- commit to save;
- switch atlas state;
- play short reveal feedback.

Coming Soon locations must look intentional and never behave like broken features.

CHAPTER 11 — PROCEDURAL GENERATION, CLEAR ZONES AND STAGES

LOCKED DECISION
Procedural decoration is deterministic and rule-driven.

Seed streams:
worldSeed, vegetationSeed, rockSeed, weatherSeed, villageDecorationSeed and stageSeed.

LOCKED DECISION — CLEAR ZONES
Important buildings, entrances and paths define exclusion areas.
Each important object may define clearRadius.
No generated tree, rock, bush, flower or decoration may enter that radius.

Apply to Home, Farm, Forester’s Hut, Mine, Harbor, Expedition Gate, future NPC homes, doors, interaction points
and central paths.

Placement checks:
- building clear radius;
- path corridor;
- minimum tree spacing;
- navigation access;
- interaction access.

Forest defaults may use 55% oak, 25% pine, 10% young tree, 5% bush and 5% stump.
Keep central navigation readable and allow denser edges.

Persistent scenes generate once and store seed plus changed object states.

Debug tools include density controls, regenerate, copy seed, clear zones, interaction ranges and collisions.

CHAPTER 12 — INPUT, INTERACTION, CAMERA AND MOBILE UI

LOCKED DECISION
Readi World is portrait-first and touch-first.

Abstract input actions:
Input.moveVector, Input.openMenu, Input.cancel, Input.contextSelect and Input.debugToggle.

LOCKED DECISION — FLOATING JOYSTICK
There is no permanent fixed joystick.
Touching the lower movement region creates the joystick at the initial touch point. Dragging controls movement.
The joystick disappears on release and may start on either side. UI touches never create movement.
Recommended movement zone: lower 55% of gameplay, excluding UI.

LOCKED DECISION — NO ACT BUTTON
There is no permanent ACT button.

Resource behavior:
- harvestable trees start chopping automatically at close range;
- compatible resource nodes may use the same logic;
- feedback is small and non-modal.

Building behavior:
- approaching shows a contextual pill;
- the pill may be tapped;
- automatic trigger is used only when accidental activation is unlikely;
- cooldown prevents repeats.

Decorative objects do not interact.

The InteractionSystem chooses one target based on distance, type, access, priority, player state and cooldown.

Camera rules:
- smooth damping;
- clamp to scene bounds;
- no empty outside space;
- logical coordinates;
- initial view includes player, Home, path and destination;
- optional subtle camera shake.

CHAPTER 13 — B+ HUD, MENU AND UI RULES

LOCKED DECISION
The approved UI direction is B+: the cozy balanced B layout combined with clear A/C time information.

Top HUD order:
- avatar;
- level and compact XP;
- wood;
- stone;
- coin;
- phase icon and name;
- world day and in-game clock;
- menu button.

HUD requirements:
- iOS safe areas;
- compact rounded translucent panels;
- readable on narrow portrait screens;
- minimal gameplay obstruction;
- no permanent object labels.

Menu opens from the top-right and currently contains Inventory, Village, Settings, Debug, Save and Coming Soon.
It does not cover the full screen, uses large touch targets and closes on outside tap.

Resource feedback examples: +3 Wood, +1 Stone.
Object names, collisions, clear zones and interaction ranges are debug-only.

CHAPTER 14 — RESOURCES, INVENTORY, ECONOMY AND MILESTONES

LOCKED DECISION
Inventory is automatic and categorized. Players do not manually move stacks.

All additions and spending pass through central services.

Economic rules:
- one ordinary currency at first;
- avoid unnecessary currencies;
- no mandatory energy wall;
- meaningful rather than arbitrary exponential costs;
- no multi-day progress loss as punishment;
- monetization must not create artificial frustration.

Milestones celebrate natural play and may reward decorations, clothing, banners, boat flags, titles or small currency.

CHAPTER 15 — AUDIO-READY ARCHITECTURE

LOCKED DECISION
Detailed sound design may come later, but every system is audio-ready.

Semantic events:

CHAPTER 16 — PERFORMANCE, RELIABILITY AND ACCESSIBILITY

LOCKED DECISION
The phone must remain responsive, cool and battery-conscious.

Targets:
- 60 FPS;
- graceful fallback;
- device pixel ratio capped at 2;
- only active scene updated;
- offscreen culling;
- minimal allocations;
- minimal DOM updates per frame;
- pause expensive work while hidden;
- autosave on backgrounding;
- avoid expensive full-screen blur.

Accessibility:
- readable text;
- large touch targets;
- no color-only information;
- reduced motion;
- camera shake toggle;
- UI scale;
- separate audio groups;
- strong contrast;
- floating joystick supports either hand.

CHAPTER 17 — LOCALIZATION AND NAMING

LOCKED DECISION
The game, code, IDs, filenames and comments are English. Design discussion may be Hungarian.

Visible text uses localization keys even when English is the only language.
Stable IDs are never renamed when display text changes.

CHAPTER 18 — SECURITY, ANTI-TAMPER AND FUTURE BACKEND

LOCKED DECISION
A fully client-side HTML game cannot honestly be called cheat-proof.

Current protection:
- validation;
- versioned saves;
- backups;
- range checks;
- clock-jump detection;
- central inventory and economy transactions;
- optional corruption checksum.

Do not waste prototype time on aggressive obfuscation, fake DRM, device locking or complex client-side encryption.

Backend becomes necessary for real-money validation, premium currency, cloud sync, accounts, leaderboards,
player trading, multiplayer or shared events.

CHAPTER 19 — ART DIRECTION AND ASSET PIPELINE

LOCKED DECISION
Approved visual direction: warm, cozy, hand-painted, clean silhouettes, soft 3D volume, lightly tilted top-down view,
soft shadows, no harsh pixel-art look, no neon palette and limited outlines.

The B+ concept is the current UI visual reference:
- compact premium top bar;
- warm dark-green translucent panels;
- clear resource icons;
- phase and clock;
- floating joystick;
- contextual world prompts;
- clear space around buildings.

Asset pipeline:
1. concept;
2. Bible check;
3. generate or draw;
4. verify perspective, scale, light and pivot;
5. arrange atlas;
6. add metadata;
7. test in engine;
8. approve and version.
